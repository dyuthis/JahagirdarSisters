ABOUT

We love music

Dyuthi Jahagirdar and Snigdha Jahagirdar are engineering students. They’ve been learning Hindustani classical music for 15 years. They’ve learned Gamaka, a unique form of Carnatic Music. Both forms of music have always been an indispensable part of their lives. Dyuthi and Snigdha were exposed to classical music from an early age, primarily through their parents, avid classical music listeners. During the first six years of their training, they learned Hindustani classical music from Shri PR Manjunath in Gwalior Gharana. They also learned from Vidushi Bharathi Prathap, a disciple of Vidushi Lalith J Rao in Agra Gharana for a year. Currently, they are getting trained under Shri Omkarnath Havaldar. They’ve learned Gamaka for 13 years, under the guidance of Smt CV Srimathi, a disciple of Padmasri Hosahalli Keshav Murthy. They consider themselves fortuitous to have found gurus who have been incredibly indulgent, systematic and, prolific in guiding them. They were also mentored for six years by Yashwant Halibandi, an acclaimed and eminent light music singer. The sisters have completed their Junior exam in Hindustani Classical Music and have been felicitated with distinction. They’re also recipients of the scholarship given by “Karnataka Sangeetha Nrithya Academy”. They have also won many awards in the district and state-level competitions. Dyuthi has won the “BALYA SINDHOORA” Award and the “CHINNARI MUTTHU” young achiever’s award from the Amma Kannada organization.
